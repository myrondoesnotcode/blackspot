FAQ

Questions, answered.

Does Blackspot upload my video anywhere?+
No. Everything runs on your Mac — no upload, no account, no servers. Your footage never leaves the machine, so unreleased cuts and clients' raw recordings stay exactly where they are.
What does Blackspot check for?+
Black frames, frozen frames, audio dropouts and silence, audio spikes, color glitches, over-exposed or washed-out sections, stray sub-second flash shots, skipped or dropped frames, and corrupt frames — plus misspelled on-screen text in titles, lower-thirds and captions. Every flag comes back with an exact timestamp.
Will it catch a single black frame?+
Yes. Detection is frame-rate aware, so a single black frame is caught whether the footage runs at 24, 25, 30 or 60 fps — the kind of one-frame flash that's almost impossible to spot by scrubbing.
What do I need to run it?+
A Mac running macOS 11 or later on Apple Silicon. Blackspot reads your export straight from disk, in place — even a multi-gigabyte 4K master — with nothing to set up.
How long does a scan take?+
One pass over the whole file — usually minutes, not the full runtime. You check a handful of timestamps instead of re-watching the entire export.
Does it work with Premiere Pro?+
Yes. A Premiere Pro panel scans your sequence, finds timeline gaps and defects, and drops a clickable marker on each — without exporting or leaving Premiere.
Can it really read on-screen text?+
Yes. Blackspot reads burned-in titles, lower-thirds and captions on-device and flags likely misspellings, so a typo in a name card gets caught before the episode goes out.
Will it flag things that are actually fine?+
Sometimes, by design. Flags are candidates, not verdicts — an intentional intro black or a static slate may show up. You confirm or dismiss each one in the built-in player; the whole idea is to check a few marks, not the entire runtime.
